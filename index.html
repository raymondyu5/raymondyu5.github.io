About Me

Hey there! I am a third year undergraduate studying computer science at the Allen School in the University of Washington, Seattle. I am fortunate to be advised by Abhishek Gupta in the WEIRD Lab. My broad research interests are in robotics, reinforcement learning, and computer vision. Recently, I've been focused on reducing the need for manual data collection to enable scalable robot learning for long-horizon, diverse, and complex tasks. At the Allen Institute for AI, I worked on the PRIOR team as a student researcher.

Alongside my research, I enjoy teaching and mentoring. I currently serve as a Teaching Assistant for CSE455 (Computer Vision). In the past I have TA'ed CSE311 (Discrete Mathematics), tutored Calculus and Linear Algebra at the UW Math Study Center, and served as a Teaching Assistant for the Saturday Enrichment Program at the UW Robinson Center, where I led sessions in neuroscience, robotics, computing, and physics.

Outside of work, I enjoy swimming, going on mindless walks, and also trying out new food. Feel free to contact me, let's chat!
